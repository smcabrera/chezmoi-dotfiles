# Interview Prompts for User Interviews Peer Reviews

## Question 1: Positive Impact & Growth

*"Highlight this person's positive impact and how you've observed this person grow and excel in the past 3-6 months."*

Use this section to highlight the meaningful ways your peer has made a positive impact over the past 3–6 months. Think about moments where their work, collaboration, or leadership strengthened the team or helped move projects forward. Consider how you've seen them grow, whether in their skills, communication, ownership, problem-solving, or the way they show up for others.

### Probing Questions for Impact

**General impact:**
- "What's the most meaningful contribution they made in the last 3-6 months?"
- "What's different now because of their work?"
- "Who benefited from their work and how? Can you quantify the impact?"
- "What would have been harder without them?"

**Specific projects:**
- "What projects did they work on? What was their role?"
- "What challenges did they navigate? How did they handle them?"
- "What decisions or technical choices did they make that stood out?"
- "What did they ship or deliver that moved things forward?"

**Collaboration & team impact:**
- "How have they strengthened the team?"
- "Describe a time they collaborated effectively with you or others—what made it work?"
- "How have they helped others? Any specific examples?"
- "What do people go to them for? What's their superpower?"

**Leadership & ownership:**
- "Where have they shown ownership or initiative?"
- "Have they stepped up during challenges or ambiguity? How?"
- "Have they mentored or helped others grow? Specific examples?"

### Probing Questions for Growth

**Observable growth:**
- "How have you seen them grow over the past 3-6 months?"
- "What could they not do 6 months ago that they can do now?"
- "What skills have they developed or improved?"
- "How has their approach or impact evolved?"

**Communication & collaboration growth:**
- "How has their communication improved?"
- "Are they collaborating differently than before? How?"
- "Have they expanded their influence or scope? In what way?"

**Problem-solving & ownership growth:**
- "How has their problem-solving approach matured?"
- "Are they taking on different types of challenges now? What kind?"
- "How have they grown in ownership or accountability?"

## Question 2: Skills or Behaviors to Develop

*"What skills or behaviors would you like to see this person acquire, develop, or refine by the next performance cycle?"*

This question is your opportunity to share feedback that supports your peer's continued growth. Consider what skills or behaviors would help them be an even stronger teammate, whether it's around collaboration, communication, problem-solving, organization, cross-functional partnership, or ownership.

### Probing Questions

**Skills to develop:**
- "What skill, if they developed it, would make them even more effective?"
- "Are there areas where they seem less confident or where they could stretch?"
- "What kinds of problems or tasks would benefit from skill development?"

**Behaviors to refine:**
- "Is there a working habit or behavior that, if adjusted, would help them be more effective?"
- "Have you observed patterns that might limit their effectiveness?"
- "What's one thing that, if they changed it, would make collaboration easier or outcomes better?"

**Communication & collaboration:**
- "How could they improve their communication or collaboration?"
- "Have there been times when communication could have been clearer or earlier?"
- "How could they more effectively share their work or gather feedback?"
- "Are there stakeholders or contexts where their communication could be stronger?"

**Ownership & scope:**
- "Where could they increase their scope, influence, or impact?"
- "What opportunities exist for them to take on more ownership or leadership?"
- "Where could they think more strategically or long-term?"

**Cross-functional partnership:**
- "How could they work more effectively with other teams or disciplines?"
- "What would help them bridge technical and non-technical contexts better?"

**The "why" behind the feedback:**
- "Why would developing this skill or behavior help them excel?"
- "How would this growth benefit them, the team, or the company?"
- "What opportunities would open up if they developed this?"

## Question 3: Additional Information & Values

*"Provide any additional information not captured in the questions above and/or describe how this person embodied User Interviews values."*

Use this space to share anything else that stood out about your experience working with this peer. This could include moments where they stepped up, supported you or the team, demonstrated strong collaboration, or brought our values to life.

### User Interviews Values

1. **People first**
2. **Fast beats perfect**
3. **Be open, be honest**
4. **Stay curious**
5. **Up and to the right**
6. **Welcome people as they are**

### Probing Questions

**Values embodiment:**
- "What's a specific moment where they lived out a company value?"
- "Which value feels most natural to them based on what you've observed?"
- "How did a value guide a decision or action they took?"
- "How have they brought values to life in their day-to-day work?"

**Stepping up:**
- "When did they step up for you or the team?"
- "Have they gone above and beyond? How?"
- "What have they done that wasn't required but made a difference?"

**Team support:**
- "How have they supported you personally?"
- "How have they contributed to team culture or morale?"
- "What makes them a good teammate?"

**Anything else:**
- "Is there anything important about working with them that the previous questions didn't capture?"
- "What do you want their manager to know about them?"
- "What would you tell someone who's about to start working with them?"

## Reframing Techniques for Peer Reviews

### From Quality to Behavior
- ❌ "Alex is a great engineer"
- ✅ "Alex consistently delivers well-tested code and proactively identifies edge cases during design discussions"

### From Vague to Specific
- ❌ "Jordan is a good communicator"
- ✅ "Jordan introduced weekly RFC reviews that helped align the team on technical decisions and unblocked two stalled projects"

### From Impression to Observable Impact
- ❌ "Taylor is helpful"
- ✅ "Taylor regularly pairs with teammates to unblock them, and created onboarding documentation that reduced new engineer ramp-up time by 2 weeks"

### From Criticism to Opportunity (Growth Areas)
- ❌ "Casey doesn't communicate enough"
- ✅ "Casey would benefit from sharing work-in-progress earlier to gather feedback before investing heavily in implementation"

- ❌ "Morgan is slow to ship"
- ✅ "Morgan could increase velocity by breaking large features into smaller, independently shippable increments"

- ❌ "Sam gets stuck in details"
- ✅ "Sam would grow by practicing when to prioritize 'good enough' solutions over perfect implementations in low-risk areas"

### From Solo Work to Collaboration Impact
- ❌ "Riley shipped the new dashboard"
- ✅ "Riley led the dashboard project, coordinated with design and product, and mentored a junior engineer throughout the implementation"

## Quantification Prompts for Peer Reviews

When the user mentions a peer's accomplishment, probe for:
- **Time**: "How much time did their work save? For whom?"
- **Scale**: "How many users, requests, or team members does their work affect?"
- **Quality**: "What metrics improved because of their work? By how much?"
- **Comparison**: "What was the situation before their contribution vs. after?"
- **Multiplier**: "Did their work make others more effective? How?"

---

## Balance & Tone Guidelines

**Specificity over platitudes**: Generic praise ("great teammate") is less valuable than specific examples ("paired with three engineers to unblock the API integration")

**Behaviors over traits**: Focus on what they *do*, not just what they *are*

**Actionable growth feedback**: Frame development areas as opportunities with clear next steps that explain the "why"

**Balanced perspective**: Question 1 is for strengths and impact, Question 2 is for constructive growth. Both are valuable.

**Professional respect**: Even when giving growth feedback, maintain a tone of respect and support

**Evidence-based**: Root all feedback in observable behaviors and concrete examples, not assumptions

**Growth-oriented**: Frame Question 2 as opportunities that will help them excel, not criticisms

---

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

❌ **Too vague**: "Great engineer, very helpful"
✅ **Specific**: "Led the chat architecture rewrite, reducing codebase complexity by 20x"

❌ **Only praise**: Skipping Question 2 or giving generic growth feedback
✅ **Balanced**: Meaningful strengths in Q1 AND actionable development areas in Q2

❌ **Criticism without direction or "why"**: "Needs to improve communication"
✅ **Actionable with context**: "Would benefit from documenting technical decisions in team Slack for visibility, which would help keep stakeholders aligned and reduce duplicate questions"

❌ **Assumed intentions**: "Doesn't care about code quality"
✅ **Observable behavior**: "Could increase test coverage, particularly for edge cases, which would reduce production bugs and build confidence in releases"

❌ **Comparing to others**: "Not as strong as Sarah at frontend work"
✅ **Individual growth**: "Could strengthen frontend skills through pairing or focused practice, which would enable them to take on more full-stack features independently"

❌ **Growth feedback that's really praise**: "Keep doing what you're doing"
✅ **Real opportunity**: "Could expand impact by sharing learnings more broadly through tech talks or documentation"
