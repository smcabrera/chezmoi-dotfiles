# Peer Review Guidance - User Interviews

## Introduction

Peer feedback is your chance to recognize a colleague's contributions over the past six months and help them grow. Your insights highlight their achievements, support their development, and strengthen your working relationship.

We know giving feedback can feel tricky - how honest is too honest? Thoughtful, constructive feedback benefits not just your colleague, but the team as a whole, creating a stronger, more collaborative environment.

Take a moment to reflect on your colleague's work, impact, and your shared experiences. Your feedback helps the team and organization thrive by promoting openness, understanding, and growth.

---

## 1. Understand Their Role

Before giving feedback, make sure you clearly understand your peer's responsibilities and the scope of their work.

**Think about:**

- What are their main contributions to the team?
- How does their role support team and organizational goals?
- Are there accomplishments or tasks they handle that others might not be aware of?

---

## 2. Impact: Reflect on Their Contributions

Consider how your peer's work has influenced the team, projects, and outcomes over the past six months. Focus on their actions, behaviors, and collaborative efforts.

**Guiding questions:**

- Which contributions had the most positive impact on the team or a project?
- How have they supported team goals or helped others succeed?
- Can you highlight any teamwork or collaborative successes?
- How do they handle challenges or setbacks to keep the team moving forward?
- What unique strengths or perspectives do they bring?

---

## 3. Development & Goal Setting

Identify areas where your peer could grow. Offer constructive suggestions that are specific, actionable, and considerate.

**Guiding questions:**

- Are there areas where they could improve to better support the team?
- What new skills or behaviors would help them succeed?
- How could they refine their approach to challenges or collaboration?
