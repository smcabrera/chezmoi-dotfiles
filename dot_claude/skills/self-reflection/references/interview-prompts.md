# Interview Prompts for User Interviews Performance Review

## Question 1: Key Achievements & Contributions

### Strengths and Contributions
*"What strengths have you consistently brought to your role? How have these positively influenced your team, department, or the company?"*

Probing questions:
- "What do people come to you for? What's your superpower on the team?"
- "Think about a recent win—what strength of yours made that possible?"
- "How has that strength rippled out to help others or improve outcomes?"

### Time Management and Prioritization
*"How have you managed your time and balanced competing priorities? Share examples of how you've stayed focused and delivered results."*

Probing questions:
- "When you had multiple urgent things competing for attention, how did you decide what to focus on?"
- "What's an example of something you deliberately deprioritized or said no to?"
- "How do you protect time for deep work vs. reactive work?"

### Key Achievements
*"What projects, actions, or initiatives are you most proud of? How have you contributed to advancing team, departmental, or company-wide goals?"*

Probing questions:
- "What shipped? What's different now because of your work?"
- "Who benefited and how? Can you quantify the impact?"
- "What would have happened if you hadn't done this work?"
- "What made this harder than it sounds?"
- "What decisions did you make that someone more junior couldn't have?"

### Feedback and Growth
*"How do you typically respond to feedback? Share any ways you've used feedback to grow or improve your performance."*

Probing questions:
- "What's a piece of feedback you received that stuck with you?"
- "How did you act on it? What changed?"
- "Is there feedback you disagreed with? How did you handle that?"

### Communication and Collaboration
*"How have you contributed to effective communication or collaboration, whether within your team or across departments?"*

Probing questions:
- "Who did you need to align or coordinate with outside your immediate team?"
- "How did you communicate complex technical work to non-technical stakeholders?"
- "What's an example of a collaboration challenge you navigated?"
- "How have you helped your team communicate or work together better?"

## Question 2: Areas for Improvement

### Skill Development
*"What technical or role-specific skills would you like to strengthen?"*

Probing questions:
- "What skill, if you had it, would make you significantly more effective?"
- "What do you find yourself avoiding or delegating because you're less confident in it?"

### Knowledge Expansion
*"What knowledge or industry insights would help you excel in your role?"*

Probing questions:
- "Where do you feel like you're operating with incomplete information?"
- "What domain knowledge would help you make better decisions?"

### Behavioral Growth
*"Are there habits like time management, adaptability, or accountability you want to improve?"*

Probing questions:
- "What habit or pattern do you wish you could change?"
- "What feedback have you received more than once?"

### Communication
*"How can you strengthen the way you present ideas or share updates?"*

Probing questions:
- "When has a message not landed the way you intended?"
- "What kind of communication feels hardest for you?"

### Leadership and Collaboration
*"How might you take more initiative or more actively support team goals?"*

Probing questions:
- "Where could you step up more?"
- "What's holding you back from leading in a particular area?"

### Process Improvement
*"Are there workflow inefficiencies you'd like to address?"*

Probing questions:
- "What process frustrates you or your team?"
- "What's something you do repeatedly that could be automated or streamlined?"

## Question 3: Values

*"Share how you've personally embodied User Interviews' values."*

Probing questions:
- "What's a specific moment where you lived out a company value?"
- "Which value feels most natural to you? Which is hardest?"
- "How did a value guide a decision you made?"

## Reframing Techniques

### From Passive to Active
- ❌ "The CI pipeline was improved"
- ✅ "I reduced CI build time from 13 minutes to 5 minutes by implementing parallel test execution"

### From Task to Impact
- ❌ "I worked on the billing system"
- ✅ "I architected a ledger-based billing system that enabled accurate revenue tracking and eliminated manual reconciliation"

### From Solo to Leadership
- ❌ "I fixed bugs"
- ✅ "I led the team's technical approach to stability, reducing production incidents by 40%"

### From Vague to Specific
- ❌ "Improved team communication"
- ✅ "Introduced weekly architecture reviews that reduced cross-team dependencies and unblocked two stalled projects"

## Quantification Prompts
When the user mentions an accomplishment, probe for:
- **Time**: "How much time does this save? Per day/week/month?"
- **Scale**: "How many users/requests/records does this affect?"
- **Money**: "What's the revenue/cost impact?"
- **Quality**: "What metrics improved? By how much?"
- **Comparison**: "What was it before vs. after?"

## Tone Guidelines
- Confident, not arrogant: State facts and impact without excessive hedging
- Specific, not vague: Use concrete examples and numbers
- Collaborative, not solo: Acknowledge team contributions while owning your role
- Growth-oriented: Frame challenges as learning opportunities
